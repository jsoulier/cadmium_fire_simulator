fuel model backends:
esa worldcover (current) sucks: 11 land cover classes crosswalked to ~8 fuel
models, no canopy data. biggest accuracy limitation rn. a backend fits if it's
a gdal-readable raster over http (COG on s3 = /vsicurl works) + a PostProcess
crosswalk to FireFuelModelType.
- Copernicus LC100 (global) -- 23 classes @ 100m + fractional cover
  (tree/shrub/grass %). best global upgrade over esa. coarser tho.
- CORINE (europe) -- 44 classes @ 100m, way finer forest types. europe only.
- NLCD (US) -- 30m ~16 classes, has fuel crosswalks. weaker than landfire.
- ESRI 10m / dynamic world -- 9 classes, fewer than esa. skip.
plan: add LANDFIRE + default for US. keep esa as global fallback. maybe LC100
for better global. NRCan for canada.
